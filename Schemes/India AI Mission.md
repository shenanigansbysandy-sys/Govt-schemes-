# India AI Mission

## Overview
India's **national artificial intelligence mission** to build a comprehensive AI ecosystem encompassing **compute infrastructure, datasets, research, skilling, and ethical AI deployment** across governance, healthcare, agriculture, education, and languages.

## Key Pillars
| Pillar | Detail | Budget |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **AI Compute Infrastructure** | 10,000+ GPUs for AI model training | ₹4,000+ crore |
| **India Datasets Platform** | Open government datasets for AI training | ₹2,000 crore |
| **AI Research & Innovation** | AI research centres, PhD fellowships | ₹2,000 crore |
| **AI Skilling** | AI/ML courses at school & college level | ₹1,000 crore |
| **AI in Governance** | AI-based citizen services (multilingual, grievance) | ₹1,000 crore |
| **Ethical AI** | Framework for responsible AI, bias mitigation | ₹500 crore |

## Challenges
- **Compute access gap** — India has very limited GPU capacity compared to US/China
- **Talent shortage** — <2% of global AI researchers are in India
- **Data quality** — Government datasets are often incomplete, unlabeled, and non-interoperable
- **Power costs** — AI compute is energy-intensive; India's electricity costs & reliability issues
- **Regulation** — No AI-specific law; draft Digital India Act covers AI but not comprehensive
- **Bias in AI** — Indian AI models can perpetuate caste, gender, & language biases
