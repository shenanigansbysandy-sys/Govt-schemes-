# ADITI (Acing Development of Innovative Technologies)

## Overview
A pioneering scheme to **promote innovation in defence technology** by supporting **startups and MSMEs** developing critical & emerging defence technologies, providing them with funding, mentoring, and pathway to procurement.

## Key Details
- **Target:** 500+ defence-tech startups & MSMEs
- **Technology Areas:** AI/ML, drones & counter-drones, robotics, cybersecurity, quantum tech, advanced materials, EW systems, loitering munitions

## Development Process
| Stage | Activity |
|-------|----------|
| **1. Problem Definition** | Armed forces identify technology challenges |
| **2. Idea Call** | Startups/MSMEs submit solutions |
| **3. Prototyping Grant** | Up to ₹10 crore per project |
| **4. Testing & Validation** | Defence labs evaluate (DRDO, DPSUs) |
| **5. Procurement** | Successful products fast-tracked into procurement |
| **6. Export potential** | Non-lethal technologies eligible for export promotion |

## Challenges
- **Long gestation** — Defence product development & certification takes 3-7 years
- **Funding gaps beyond prototype** — "Valley of death" between prototype grant & bulk procurement
- **Defence procurement complexity** — DPP (Defence Procurement Procedure) is still complex despite iDEX reforms
- **Import dependency** — Critical defence components (sensors, chips) still imported
- **Risk aversion** — Armed forces hesitant to adopt unproven startup technology
