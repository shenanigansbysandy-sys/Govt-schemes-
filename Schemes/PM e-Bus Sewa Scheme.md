# PM e-Bus Sewa Scheme

## Overview
A scheme to deploy **10,000+ electric buses** in **100+ cities** across India through the **PPP (Public-Private Partnership) model**, providing **clean, electric public transport** to urban populations.

## Key Details
- **Target:** 10,000 e-buses in cities without organized bus services (tier-2/3 cities)
- **Model:** PPP — Private operators purchase & operate buses; government provides viability gap funding
- **Infrastructure:** Charging depots & infrastructure included
- **Coverage:** 100+ cities across states & UTs
- **Budget:** ₹20,000+ crore (Central + State + Private)

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Cities identified | ✅ 100+ cities selected |
| Buses tendered | ✅ 5,000+ tendered |
| Buses operational | 🔄 2,500+ on road |
| Charging stations | 🔄 1,500+ charging points installed |
| States onboarded | ✅ 15 states signed MoUs |

## Challenges
- **High upfront cost** — E-buses cost ₹1.5-2 crore vs ₹50 lakh for diesel buses
- **Charging infrastructure** — Insufficient grid capacity in many cities for fleet charging
- **Range anxiety** — E-buses have limited range (150-200 km) — unsuitable for long routes
- **Maintenance skills** — Lack of trained mechanics for e-bus maintenance
- **State financial health** — Many states struggle to pay their share of Viability Gap Funding
