# National Manufacturing Mission

## Overview
Announced in **Budget 2025-26**, this mission aims to enhance India's **manufacturing capacity and competitiveness** by focusing on **future technologies, MSME support, skilling, and ease of doing business** — complementing PLI & Make in India.

## Key Details
- **Six Pillars:**
  1. **Future Technology Manufacturing** — Semiconductors, EV batteries, solar cells, AI hardware, space components
  2. **MSME Strengthening** — Technology upgrade, quality certification, zero-defect manufacturing
  3. **Industrial Parks & Clusters** — 12 new industrial smart cities, plug-and-play parks
  4. **Skilling for Industry 4.0** — AI, IoT, robotics, 3D printing skilling at ITI & polytechnic level
  5. **Ease of Doing Business 2.0** — Single-window clearance, trust-based compliance, removal of outdated laws
  6. **Green Manufacturing** — Decarbonizing industrial processes, green hydrogen in steel & chemicals

## Challenges
- **China dominance** — 35% of global manufacturing is in China; India's share is ~3% — massive gap
- **Land fragmentation** — Large contiguous industrial land parcels are scarce & expensive
- **Labour reform** — Despite new labour codes, state-level implementation is patchy
- **Power quality** — Manufacturing requires reliable power; many states have frequent cuts
- **Export competitiveness** — India's manufacturing exports are constrained by logistics costs, tariffs, and quality perception
