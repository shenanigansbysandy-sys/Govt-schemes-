# National Logistics Policy (NLP)

## Overview
A comprehensive policy framework to **reduce India's logistics cost** from **14% of GDP to under 10%** by 2030, improve the **Logistics Performance Index (LPI)** ranking, and create a **data-driven, digitally-enabled logistics ecosystem**.

## Key Pillars
| Pillar | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Integrated Digital Platform** (ULIP) | Unified Logistics Interface Platform — single window for logistics data |
| **Process Re-engineering** | Standardization of documentation, digitization of customs & port processes |
| **Logistics Parks** | Multi-modal logistics parks in strategic locations (MMLPs) |
| **Skill Development** | Logistics-focused skill certification under PMKVY |
| **State Engagement** | State Logistics Policies — integrated action plans |

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| ULIP portal launched | ✅ Operational — integrating 30+ IT systems |
| Logistics cost reduction | 🔄 Estimated at ~13% of GDP (from 14%) |
| LPI ranking | 📈 Improved from 44 (2018) to ~32 (2026) |
| State logistics policies | 🔄 20+ states have formulated |
| MMLP development | 🔄 Multiple logistics parks under development |
| Unified docs | ✅ E-way bill, e-invoice, import/export digitized |

## Challenges
- **Fragmented sector** — Logistics involves 20+ government agencies, 200+ processes
- **Data sharing reluctance** — Private players hesitant to share logistics data on ULIP
- **Trucking inefficiency** — 80% of freight moves by road; truck utilization only 60% (empty return trips)
- **State implementation gaps** — State-level policy adoption slow in smaller states
- **Warehousing** — Lack of grade-A warehousing; most are small, fragmented, and unorganized
