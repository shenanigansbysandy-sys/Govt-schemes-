# Institutions of Eminence (IoE) Scheme

## Overview
A scheme to **transform 20 higher education institutions** (public & private) into **world-class teaching & research universities**, providing them with **enhanced autonomy** and **additional funding**.

## IoE Tagged Institutions
| Category | Institutions |
|----------|-------------|
| **Public (10)** | IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Madras, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Kanpur, IISc Bangalore, Delhi University, BHU, JNU, Hyderabad University |
| **Private (10)** | BITS Pilani, Manipal Academy, VIT, Amrita Vishwa, Jio Institute, Shiv Nadar, etc. |

## Key Benefits
- **Academic autonomy** — Freedom to set curriculum, admission criteria, fees
- **Financial autonomy** — Enhanced budgetary support + ability to raise funds
- **Regulatory relaxation** — Exemptions from UGC/AICTE regulations
- **International collaboration** — Ease of hiring foreign faculty & enrolling foreign students

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| IoEs declared | ✅ 20 institutions tagged |
| Fund utilization | 🔄 ₹6,000+ crore utilized for faculty recruitment & research |
| Faculty-student ratio | 📈 Improved from 1:20 to 1:12 in public IoEs |
| International rankings | 📈 Several IoEs improved QS/THE rankings year-on-year |
| Global collaborations | ✅ 100+ MoUs with top global universities |

## Challenges
- **Regulatory resistance** — UGC & AICTE still impose bureaucracy despite promised autonomy
- **Faculty shortage** — Difficulty attracting & retaining world-class faculty
- **Infrastructure gaps** — Public IoEs have aging infrastructure needing upgrade
- **Financial constraints** — Government funding not at par with global peers
- **Research output** — Still far behind top global universities in citation indices & patents
