# Ayushman Arogya Mandir (AAM)

## Overview
A network of **1.5+ lakh Ayushman Arogya Mandirs** (previously Health & Wellness Centres) being established across India to deliver **comprehensive primary healthcare** closer to communities — covering preventive, promotive, curative, and rehabilitative services.

## Key Details
- **Target:** 1.5 lakh centres operational (from existing 30,000 SCs/PHCs)
- **Services:** 12 packages including maternal & child health, NCD screening, mental health, dental, ENT, ophthalmic care
- **Key Innovation:** Tele-consultation through **eSanjeevani** platform at each AAM
- **Focus:** **Community outreach** — ASHA & ANM workers form the frontline

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Centres operational | ✅ 1.7+ lakh Ayushman Arogya Mandirs established (exceeded target) |
| Tele-consultations | ✅ 15+ crore eSanjeevani consultations via AAMs |
| NCD screening | ✅ Hypertension & diabetes screening for 30+ crore adults |
| Free medicines & diagnostics | 🔄 Available at ~80% of centres — gaps in supply chain remain |
| Mental health services | 🔄 Being rolled out — trained counselors deployed |

## Challenges
- **Infrastructure gaps** — Many AAMs operate from rented/built-up spaces without adequate equipment
- **Staff shortages** — Shortage of doctors, nurses, lab technicians at PHC level (~20% vacancies)
- **Supply chain** — Inconsistent availability of free medicines & diagnostic tests
- **Quality variability** — Huge variance in service quality across states (Tamil Nadu/Kerala good, others lag)
- **Community awareness** — Many rural residents unaware of expanded services at AAMs
