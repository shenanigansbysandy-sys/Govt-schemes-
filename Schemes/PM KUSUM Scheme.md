# PM KUSUM Scheme (Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan)

## Overview
A transformative scheme that promotes **solar energy adoption in agriculture** by helping farmers install **solar pumps** and **solar power plants** on their barren/fallow land, providing energy security & additional income.

## Three Components
| Component | Detail | Target |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Component A** | 10,000 MW grid-connected solar plants on barren land | Farmers install 0.5-2 MW solar plants & sell power to DISCOMs |
| **Component B** | 20+ lakh standalone solar pumps | Replaces diesel pumps with solar (₹0 operating cost) |
| **Component C** | 15+ lakh grid-connected solar pumps | Farmers feed surplus power to grid & earn |

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Solar pumps installed | ✅ 8+ lakh (Components B+C) |
| Grid-connected solar plants | 🔄 ~2,000 MW capacity commissioned (Component A) |
| Diesel replaced | 📈 ~500 crore litres of diesel saved annually |
| Farmer income | 📈 Farmers earning ₹50,000-₹1 lakh/year from power sales |
| State adoption | ✅ Leading states: Rajasthan, Maharashtra, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu |

## Challenges
- **High upfront cost** — Even with 60% subsidy, farmers need to invest ₹2-3 lakh per pump
- **DISCOM cooperation** — Many DISCOMs reluctant to sign PPAs for power purchase from farmers
- **Land requirement** — Component A requires 4-8 acres of land — not feasible for small farmers
- **Maintenance** — Solar panels & pumps require technical maintenance; limited rural service network
- **Water sustainability** — Solar pumps can lead to over-extraction of groundwater (no ongoing cost for pumping)
