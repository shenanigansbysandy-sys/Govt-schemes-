# Women in Tech and STEM Mission

## Overview
A new mission launched in **Budget 2025-26** to **promote participation of girls and women** in **Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM)** careers, addressing the significant drop-off of women from STEM fields after graduation.

## Key Details
- **Context:** India has 43% women STEM graduates (highest globally) but only **14% women in STEM workforce** — massive leaky pipeline
- **Components:**
  1. **STEM Scholarships** for meritorious girls from low-income families to pursue higher education
  2. **Mentorship Programme** — Women scientists/engineers mentoring school & college girls
  3. **Research Fellowships** — Post-doc fellowships specifically for women
  4. **Tech Skilling** — Coding, AI, data science courses for women (in collaboration with tech companies)
  5. **Return-to-work** — Mid-career re-entry programs for women after career breaks
  6. **Awareness** — Breaking gender stereotypes about STEM careers among parents & teachers

## Challenges
- **Societal bias** — Deep-rooted patriarchal attitudes regarding women's roles & careers
- **Safety concerns** — Women in tech face workplace harassment; night shifts for women discouraged
- **Career break penalty** — Women who take breaks face severe career progression penalties
- **Lack of role models** — Few women leaders in STEM — absence breeds absence
- **Gap between education & employment** — STEM curriculum not aligned with industry needs
