# DDU-GKY (Deendayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushalya Yojana)

## Overview
A flagship **rural youth skilling & placement scheme** that provides **residential skill training** with guaranteed **placement** for rural poor youth aged 15-35 years.

## Key Details
- **Target:** Rural youth from BPL (Below Poverty Line) families, SC/ST, women
- **Training:** 3-12 months residential training in sector-specific skills
- **Sectors Covered:** Retail, hospitality, healthcare, construction, automotive, IT, BFSI, logistics, green jobs
- **Guarantee:** 75% placement guarantee with minimum wage assurance
- **Post-placement support:** 3-6 months counselling after placement

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Youth trained | ✅ 15+ lakh |
| Placement achieved | ✅ 10+ lakh (70% placement rate) |
| Training partners | ✅ 1,000+ Project Implementing Agencies (PIAs) |
| Trades offered | ✅ 200+ job roles across 30+ sectors |
| Women participation | ✅ ~45% of trainees |

## Challenges
- **Placement quality** — Many placed in low-wage jobs (₹8,000-12,000/month) — not aspirational
- **Dropout during training** — ~25% dropout due to cultural adjustment to residential training
- **Industry relevance** — Training not always aligned with actual market demand
- **Retention post-placement** — ~40% leave jobs within 6 months due to homesickness or low wages
- **Geographical imbalance** — Most training centres in South/West; NE & East underserved
