# Make in India

## Overview
A national program launched by PM Modi to **transform India into a global manufacturing hub** by promoting investment, fostering innovation, building infrastructure, and improving ease of doing business across 25+ sectors.

## Key Sectors (25+)
Automobiles, Aviation, Biotechnology, Chemicals, Construction, Defence Manufacturing, Electrical Machinery, Electronic Systems, Food Processing, IT & BPM, Leather, Media & Entertainment, Mining, Oil & Gas, Pharmaceuticals, Ports & Shipping, Railways, Renewable Energy, Roads & Highways, Space, Textiles, Thermal Power, Tourism & Hospitality, Wellness

## Key Achievements
| Parameter | 2014 | 2025-26 |
|-----------|------|---------|
| FDI inflows | $45 billion | $80+ billion/year |
| Ease of Doing Business rank | 142 | 63 |
| GST implementation | ❌ No | ✅ Yes (unified market) |
| Corporate tax rate | 30% | 22% |
| IBC (Insolvency Code) | ❌ No | ✅ Operational |
| Manufacturing PMI | 50 | 56+ (expansion) |

## Key Reforms Enabled
- **GST** — One nation, one tax (2017)
- **IBC** — Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code (2016)
- **Corporate tax reduction** — 22% base rate (2019)
- **FDI liberalization** — 100% in most sectors
- **Intellectual property** — Patent application processing reduced from 5 yrs to 18 months
- **Defence production** — Positive indigenization lists (4,000+ items banned for import)

## Challenges
- **Manufacturing GDP share stagnant** — 17% of GDP (target was 25%) — largely unchanged since 2014
- **China+1 opportunity partially captured** — Vietnam, Thailand, Mexico competing harder
- **Labour law complexity** — despite 4 new labour codes, implementation is slow
- **Land acquisition** — remains a major hurdle for large manufacturing projects
- **Skill gap** — only 5% of Indian workforce formally skilled vs. 50%+ in China & Korea
- **Export stagnation** — India's share in global exports is 1.7% (virtually unchanged)
