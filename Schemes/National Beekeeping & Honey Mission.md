# National Beekeeping & Honey Mission (NBHM)

## Overview
A mission to promote **beekeeping as a sustainable livelihood** for rural communities, increase **honey production**, and boost crop **pollination** for higher agricultural yields under the **Sweet Revolution** initiative.

## Key Details
- **Objective:** Triple honey production & double beekeepers' income by 2030
- **Components:** Training, bee colony distribution, modern equipment, processing infrastructure, marketing support
- **Current Production:** ~1.3 lakh MT annually (5th largest in the world)
- **Export Potential:** Indian honey exported to 50+ countries (USA, Canada, EU, Middle East)

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Beekeepers trained | ✅ 2+ lakh farmers trained |
| Bee colonies distributed | ✅ 10+ lakh bee colonies distributed |
| Honey processing units | 🔄 100+ units established |
| FPOs for beekeepers | 🔄 200+ Farmer Producer Organizations formed |
| Export growth | 📈 Indian honey exports growing at 12-15% annually |

## Challenges
- **Pesticide use** — Indiscriminate pesticide spraying kills bee populations
- **Climate change** — Erratic flowering patterns due to changing seasons
- **Quality standards** — Stringent EU/US quality requirements for export (antibiotics, pesticides)
- **Adulteration** — Honey adulteration damages India's export reputation
- **Access to credit** — Beekeepers often not covered under KCC or regular banking
