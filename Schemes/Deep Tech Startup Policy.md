# Deep Tech Startup Policy

## Overview
A dedicated policy & funding framework to support **deep tech startups** working in **Artificial Intelligence, Robotics, Quantum Computing, Blockchain, Advanced Materials, Biotechnology, Semiconductors, Space Tech, and Clean Energy** — areas requiring high R&D investment and long gestation periods.

## Key Details
- **Deep Tech Fund:** ₹10,000 crore corpus for deep tech startups
- **Support Structure:**
  1. **R&D Grants** — Early-stage research funding (up to ₹5 crore per startup)
  2. **Venture Debt** — Low-interest debt for deep tech commercialization
  3. **Equity Support** — Government co-investment alongside VC funds
  4. **IP Support** — Patent filing assistance & fast-track examination
  5. **Regulatory Sandbox** — Testing environment for emerging technologies
  6. **Infrastructure** — Access to government R&D labs & supercomputing
- **Target Sectors:** AI/ML, Robotics, Quantum, Semiconductors, Space Tech, Biotech, Clean Energy, Cyber Security

## Challenges
- **High risk** — Deep tech startups have high failure rates; government fund must tolerate failures
- **Long gestation** — Deep tech commercialization takes 7-10 years; patient capital is scarce
- **Talent shortage** — India lacks deep tech PhDs & specialized engineers
- **Global competition** — US, China, EU have multi-billion dollar deep tech funds
- **Regulatory uncertainty** — Emerging tech (AI, quantum, space) lacks clear regulation
- **VC ecosystem mismatch** — Indian VCs prefer asset-light tech (~IT services); deep tech needs different investors
