# Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH)

## Overview
A comprehensive scheme for the **holistic development of horticulture** in India, covering fruits, vegetables, spices, flowers, mushrooms, bamboo, and medicinal plants.

## Key Details
- **Coverage:** All states and UTs
- **Components:** Production, post-harvest management, processing, marketing
- **Sub-schemes:** 
  - National Horticulture Mission (NHM) — for states
  - Horticulture Mission for NE & Himalayan States (HMNEH)
  - National Bamboo Mission (NBM)
  - National Horticulture Board (NHB)
- **India's Horticulture Output:** ~340+ million MT annually — surpassing food grain production

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Area under horticulture | 📈 Expanded from 25M ha (2014) to 30M+ ha (2026) |
| Production | 📈 Increased from 277M MT to 340M+ MT |
| Farmer training | ✅ 30+ lakh farmers trained in horticulture practices |
| Cold storage creation | 🔄 Ongoing — 15,000+ cold storage units created |
| Export promotion | 🔒 India 2nd largest producer of fruits & vegetables globally |

## Challenges
- **Post-harvest losses** — 15-20% of horticulture produce is wasted due to inadequate cold chain
- **Price volatility** — Perishable nature leads to price crashes during bumper harvests
- **Fragmented holdings** — Small landholdings limit economies of scale
- **Quality standards** — Meeting global phyto-sanitary standards for export remains difficult
- **Irrigation access** — Only 45% of horticulture area has assured irrigation
