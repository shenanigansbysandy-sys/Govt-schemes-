# AMRUT Plan

## Overview
The **Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT)** focuses on providing **basic infrastructure** in 500+ cities and towns — water supply, sewerage, storm water drainage, urban transport, and green spaces.

## AMRUT 2.0 (launched 2021)
- **Vision:** Make cities **water-secure** and **sewage-treated** with circular economy approach
- **Key Targets:** 100% water supply coverage, 100% sewerage/septage management
- **Focus:** **Pey Jal Survekshan** — water quality & conservation rankings
- **Budget:** ₹2.77 lakh crore (₹66,750 crore Central share)

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | AMRUT 1.0 | AMRUT 2.0 |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Water tap connections | ✅ 1.2+ crore added | 🔄 ~70 lakh more targeted |
| Sewerage connections | ✅ 60+ lakh added | 🔄 Underway |
| Parks & green spaces | ✅ 2,000+ parks developed | 🔄 Additional 1,500+ planned |
| Water treatment capacity | ✅ 1,500+ MLD added | 🔄 Being expanded |

## Challenges
- **Water availability** — Many cities face source water scarcity; AMRUT creates infrastructure but water sources are depleting
- **Operation & maintenance** — ULBs struggle to maintain water & sewage treatment plants
- **Tariff resistance** — User charges are politically sensitive, making O&M financially unviable
- **Sewage coverage gap** — Only ~30% of urban sewage is treated; AMRUT 2.0 aims to double this
- **Inter-state water disputes** — Cities dependent on inter-state rivers face supply uncertainty
