# Atal Pension Yojana (APY)

## Overview
India's **largest contributory pension scheme for the unorganized sector**, providing a **guaranteed minimum monthly pension of ₹1,000-₹5,000** to subscribers aged 18-40, with **50% spouse pension** and **full contribution return** on death.

## Key Details
- **Eligibility:** Any citizen aged 18-40 years, with savings bank account
- **Pension Options:** ₹1,000/₹2,000/₹3,000/₹4,000/₹5,000 per month (after 60 years)
- **Contribution:** Fixed monthly contribution based on age at entry & pension choice (e.g., 25-year-old for ₹5,000 pension: ₹210/month)
- **Government Co-contribution:** 50% of contribution (up to ₹1,000/year) for 5 years for non-taxpayers enrolled before March 2022
- **Family pension:** 50% to spouse after subscriber's death
- **Return of corpus:** If subscriber dies after 60, remaining corpus returned to nominee
- **Enrolments:** 8+ crore (48% women) — exceeded initial target of 5 crore

## Challenges
- **Sticky auto-debit** — Many inactive accounts due to insufficient balance; auto-debit fails repeatedly
- **Awareness in rural areas** — Rural enrolments lower than urban despite unorganized workers being mostly rural
- **Competing priorities** — Multiple pension schemes (APY, PM-SYM, NPS) confuse potential subscribers
- **Pension adequacy** — ₹5,000/month pension (max) is insufficient for decent old-age income
- **Inflation erosion** — Fixed pension doesn't adjust for inflation
- **Enrolment quality** — Banks have enrolled many accounts without proper subscriber understanding
