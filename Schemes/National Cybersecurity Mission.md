# National Cybersecurity Mission

## Overview
A comprehensive mission to **strengthen India's cybersecurity posture** by building a secure digital ecosystem, developing indigenous cybersecurity products, training 5 lakh cybersecurity professionals, and creating a **National Cyber Coordination Centre (NCCC)** .

## Key Details
- **Objective:** Protect India's critical information infrastructure (CII) — banking, power, telecom, defence, health, transport
- **Five Pillars:**
  1. **National Cyber Coordination Centre (NCCC)** — Real-time threat monitoring & response
  2. **Cyber Crisis Management Plan (CCMP)** — Coordinated response to cyber attacks
  3. **Cyber Security R&D** — Indigenous tools, encryption, quantum-safe security
  4. **Human Resource Development** — 5 lakh certified cybersecurity professionals by 2030
  5. **Cyber Hygiene Awareness** — Nationwide awareness campaigns (Cyber Surakshit Bharat)
- **Regulatory:** Draft Digital India Act with cybersecurity provisions

## Challenges
- **Global cyber threat landscape** — Ransomware, state-sponsored attacks increasing exponentially
- **Talent shortage** — Global shortage of 4M+ cybersecurity professionals; India faces acute gap
- **Indigenous technology gap** — 90%+ cybersecurity solutions currently imported
- **Coordination** — Multiple agencies (CERT-In, NCB, police, intelligence) need seamless info-sharing
- **Privacy vs. Security** — Balancing surveillance needs with citizen privacy rights
- **State-level capacity** — State police cyber cells lack technical capability & staffing
