# PM Poshan (Mid-Day Meal Scheme)

## Overview
The **world's largest school feeding program**, providing **hot cooked meals** to over **11 crore children** in government and government-aided schools, aiming to improve nutrition, school attendance, and social equity.

## Key Details
- **Coverage:** Class 1-8 students in government & government-aided schools
- **Nutrition Norms:** Minimum 450-700 calories + 12-20g protein per meal
- **Revamped (PM Poshan) 2021:** 
  - Focus on **nutritional outcomes** (micro-nutrients, fortified foods)
  - **Tithi Bhojan** — community participation in meal preparation
  - **School Kitchen Gardens** — vegetables grown by students
  - **Holistic nutrition** — millet-based meals, nutritional counseling

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Students covered | ✅ 11+ crore daily |
| Schools covered | ✅ 11.5+ lakh |
| Cook-cum-helpers | ✅ 25+ lakh employed (majority women) |
| School kitchen gardens | 🔄 5+ lakh gardens established |
| Revised menu (millets included) | 🔄 Rolled out in 15 states |

## Challenges
- **Quality & variety** — Meals often lack nutritional diversity (same menu daily)
- **Caste discrimination** — In some regions, Dalit cooks & children face discrimination during meals
- **Corruption & leakage** — Diversion of funds & grains reported in several states
- **Cooking infrastructure** — Many schools lack proper kitchen sheds, utensils, & clean water
- **Pandemic disruption** — COVID caused 2-year break; re-enrolment & nutrition recovery challenging
