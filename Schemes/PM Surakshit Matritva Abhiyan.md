# PM Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA)

## Overview
A campaign providing **high-quality antenatal care** to pregnant women on the **9th of every month** at government health facilities, ensuring high-risk pregnancies are identified & managed promptly.

## Key Details
- **Objective:** Reduce maternal & infant mortality rates
- **Service:** Free antenatal check-ups, ultrasound, blood tests, iron supplementation
- **Frequency:** Every 9th of the month (PMSMA Day) — specialized OB-GYN doctors available
- **Coverage:** All PHCs, CHCs, and district hospitals
- **High risk identification:** Pregnant women with complications get referrals & follow-up

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Checks performed | ✅ 5+ crore antenatal check-ups since launch |
| High risk pregnancies identified | ✅ ~15% detected as high-risk |
| Follow-up compliance | 🔄 ~70% — needs improvement |
| Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR) | 📈 Reduced from 130 (2016) to ~97 (2026) per 1,00,000 live births |

## Challenges
- **Quality variance** — Specialist doctors available only in urban/town facilities, not remote PHCs
- **Follow-up gap** — ~30% of high-risk women don't complete recommended follow-ups
- **Ultrasound access** — Limited ultrasound machines in rural PHCs
- **ASHA workload** — ASHA workers are stretched with multiple responsibilities
- **Anemia** — Over 50% of pregnant women still suffer from anemia despite iron distribution
