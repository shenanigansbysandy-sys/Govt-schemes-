# National AYUSH Mission (NAM)

## Overview
A comprehensive scheme to **promote traditional Indian medicine systems** — **Ayurveda, Yoga & Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha, Sowa-Rigpa, and Homoeopathy (AYUSH)** — by improving education, research, and healthcare delivery.

## Key Details
- **Components:** 
  - AYUSH health services (hospitals, dispensaries, drug testing labs)
  - AYUSH educational institutions (upgradation of colleges)
  - Medicinal plant conservation & cultivation
  - AYUSH research & quality control
- **Integration:** AYUSH services being mainstreamed into public health through Ayushman Arogya Mandirs

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| AYUSH hospitals supported | ✅ 3,500+ |
| AYUSH dispensaries | ✅ 27,000+ |
| AYUSH co-locations in PHCs | ✅ 12,000+ PHCs have AYUSH facilities |
| Medicinal plant cultivation | 🔄 1.5+ lakh hectares under cultivation |
| AYUSH research | 🔄 100+ clinical trials underway |

## Challenges
- **Quality standardization** — Lack of uniform quality standards across AYUSH products & practices
- **Integration gaps** — Allopathic doctors often resist AYUSH integration at PHCs
- **Scientific validation** — Need for more rigorous clinical evidence for AYUSH treatments
- **Regulatory gaps** — AYUSH drug regulation is weaker compared to allopathic drugs
- **Awareness** — Limited awareness of AYUSH services among general public
