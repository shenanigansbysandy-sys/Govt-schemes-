# Aspirational Districts Programme (ADP)

## Overview
A **transformative monitoring & ranking framework** by NITI Aayog to rapidly improve **socio-economic outcomes** in India's **112 most backward districts** by driving convergence of schemes, data-based monitoring, and competitive ranking.

## Key Details
- **Coverage:** 112 aspirational districts across all states
- **Selection:** Composite index of backwardness (education, health, infrastructure, economic)
- **49 Indicators across 6 themes:** 
  1. Health & Nutrition (30% weight)
  2. Education (30%)
  3. Agriculture & Water Resources (20%)
  4. Financial Inclusion (10%)
  5. Skill Development (5%)
  6. Basic Infrastructure (5%)
- **Key Innovation:** Monthly delta ranking (districts ranked on improvement speed)
- **Model:** **Collector-led convergence** — District Collector given flexibility to deploy CSS funds based on local needs

## Key Achievements (2018-2026)

|-----------|-----------------|---------------|-------------|

| Toilet access | 40% | 85% | ✅ +45% |

## Challenges
- **Ranking vs. reality** — Rankings are based on self-reported data — risk of data fudging
- **Not all districts progressed equally** — Some districts (especially NE & conflict-affected) show minimal improvement
- **Sustainability** — Once ADP phase ends, districts may slip back without continued pressure
- **Collector rotation** — High-performing Collectors transferred frequently — disrupts momentum
- **Data gaps** — Some indicators lack reliable baseline & tracking data
