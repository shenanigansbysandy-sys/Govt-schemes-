# National Digital Health Mission (NDHM) / ABDM

## Overview
A digital health ecosystem that creates **unique health IDs (Ayushman Bharat Health Account — ABHA)** for every citizen, linking their medical records digitally, enabling **portable, interoperable health data** across the country.

## Key Components
| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **ABHA Number** | 14-digit unique health ID — 50+ crore created |
| **Health Records** | Digital records linked to ABHA (DigiLocker integration) |
| **Health Facility Registry** | 2+ lakh hospitals, clinics, labs registered |
| **Health Professional Registry** | 5+ lakh doctors/nurses registered |
| **Consent Manager** | Patient-controlled data sharing through consent |

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| ABHA IDs created | ✅ 60+ crore |
| Health facilities registered | ✅ 2.5+ lakh |
| Health professionals registered | ✅ 6+ lakh |
| Health records linked | 🔄 ~30 crore records — growing steadily |
| Private hospital integration | 🔄 Major hospital chains (Apollo, Fortis) onboarded |
| Public health programme linkage | 🔄 Integration with Ayushman Bharat, PM-JAY underway |

## Challenges
- **Data privacy concerns** — Digital health data security & misuse are major citizen concerns
- **Low adoption among providers** — Small clinics & nursing homes hesitant to join
- **Interoperability** — Disparate IT systems across states & hospitals create integration challenges
- **Digital literacy** — Elderly & rural populations struggle with digital health systems
- **No legal framework** — Digital health data protection bill still in progress
