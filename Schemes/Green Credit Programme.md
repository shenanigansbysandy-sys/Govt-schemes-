# Green Credit Programme

## Overview
An innovative market-based mechanism where **Green Credits** (tradable) are awarded to individuals, communities, or entities for **environment-positive actions** — tree plantation, water conservation, waste management, air pollution reduction, etc.

## Key Details
- **How it Works:** Environmental actions → Verified credits → Traded on Green Credit platform
- **Eligible Activities:**
  1. Tree plantation & afforestation
  2. Water conservation & rainwater harvesting
  3. Waste management (segregation, recycling)
  4. Air pollution reduction
  5. Mangrove & wetland restoration
  6. Sustainable agriculture practices
- **Platform:** Digital Green Credit Registry (linked with government & corporate buyers)
- **Difference from Carbon Credits:** Green Credits = broader environmental actions (not just carbon)

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Pilot phase | 🔄 Launched in 10 districts |

| International interest | 🔄 Subject to bilateral recognition agreements |

## Challenges
- **Double counting risk** — Same action could generate both Green & Carbon credits
- **Verification** — Ensuring additionality and preventing fraud in credit generation
- **Market demand** — Corporate adoption is voluntary; no mandatory green credit obligation
- **Pricing mechanism** — No floor price; market discovery yet to stabilize
- **Integration with global markets** — Not yet linked with international carbon markets (Article 6)
