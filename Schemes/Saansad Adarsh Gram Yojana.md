# Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY)

## Overview
A unique scheme where **Members of Parliament (MPs)** adopt a **Gram Panchayat** and lead its holistic development — making it a **model village** with improved infrastructure, social harmony, economic opportunities, and governance.

## Key Details
- **Target:** Each MP adopts 1 gram panchayat every year (Lok Sabha: 1 + Rajya Sabha: 1)
- **Focus Areas:** 
  - Basic infrastructure (roads, water, electricity, sanitation)
  - Social development (education, health, nutrition)
  - Economic development (livelihoods, skill training)
  - Social harmony (caste/gender equity, community participation)
  - Good governance (digital services, transparency)
- **Funding:** Convergence of existing schemes + MPLAD funds + CSR

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Gram Panchayats adopted | ✅ 10,000+ (cumulative) |
| Fully developed | ✅ ~3,000 reached "Adarsh" status |
| MPs actively participating | 🔄 ~75% MPs engaged (some inactive) |
| Funding through convergence | ✅ ₹500+ crore mobilized through MPLAD & CSS convergence |
| Notable examples | ✅ Hiware Bazar (Ahmednagar) — model replicated |

## Challenges
- **MP interest variance** — Scheme success depends entirely on MP's personal interest
- **Definition of "Adarsh"** — No clear, measurable criteria for what constitutes Adarsh Gram
- **MP replacement** — When MP changes (elections), new MP may not continue adopted village
- **Sustainability** — Many villages developed but lack maintenance after initial push
- **Overlap with other schemes** — Not clear additional value over regular CSS (PMGSY, PMAY, etc.)
- **Political color** — Some MPs focus on party strongholds rather than deserving villages
