# SWAMIH Fund

## Overview
The **Special Window for Affordable and Mid-Income Housing (SWAMIH)** fund is a **last-mile funding** vehicle to complete **stressed residential housing projects** that are stuck due to lack of funding, providing relief to **middle-class homebuyers**.

## Key Details
- **SWAMIH 1 (2019):** ₹15,000 crore fund — 80% of projects completed/on track
- **SWAMIH 2 (2025):** New fund announced in Budget 2025-26 to complete additional stressed projects
- **Target:** ~1 lakh middle-income housing units (~5 lakh families benefitted)
- **Focus:** Stalled projects in tier-1, tier-2 & tier-3 cities

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | SWAMIH 1 | SWAMIH 2 |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Projects sanctioned | ✅ 160+ projects approved | 🔄 Fund structure being finalized |
| Homes completed/delivered | ✅ 35,000+ units delivered | 🔄 Yet to commence disbursement |
| Investment disbursed | ✅ ₹12,000+ crore deployed | 🆕 New |
| Homebuyers benefitted | ✅ 1.5+ lakh families | 🆕 Targeting 5 lakh families |

## Challenges
- **Legal complexity** — Many stalled projects involve litigation (NCLT, RERA, consumer courts)
- **Developer defaults** — Some developers have absconded or lack intent to complete
- **Cost overruns** — Delays have significantly increased construction costs
- **Homebuyer trust** — Buyer confidence is low after years of uncertainty
- **Coordination** — Requires coordination between banks, developers, homebuyers, and regulators
