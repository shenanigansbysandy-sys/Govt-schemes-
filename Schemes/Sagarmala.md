# Sagarmala

## Overview
India's **port modernization & coastal development program** aimed at reducing logistics costs by optimizing port infrastructure, enhancing connectivity, and promoting **port-led industrialization**.

## Key Pillars
| Pillar | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Port Modernization** | Upgrading 12 major ports + 200+ non-major ports |
| **Port Connectivity** | Rail/road/waterway connectivity from ports to hinterland |
| **Port-led Industrialization** | Coastal Economic Zones (CEZs) near ports |
| **Coastal Community Development** | Livelihood for coastal communities, skill training |
| **Inland Waterways** | Cargo movement via National Waterways (cost-effective & eco-friendly) |

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Total projects | ✅ 800+ identified (₹8+ lakh crore investment) |
| Projects completed | ✅ 300+ |
| Projects under implementation | 🔄 250+ |
| Port capacity | 📈 Increased from 1,200 MTPA (2015) to 2,600+ MTPA (2026) |
| Turnaround time | 📈 Reduced from 4+ days to ~1 day at major ports |
| Inland waterway cargo | 📈 Increased 5x (from 70 MT to 350+ MT annually) |

## Challenges
- **State cooperation** — Port-led industrialization requires state-level land & policy support
- **Environmental concerns** — Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) norms & mangrove destruction issues
- **Rail connectivity gaps** — Last-mile rail connectivity from ports to hinterland still missing
- **Dredging challenges** — Many ports require regular dredging to handle larger vessels
- **Inter-ministerial coordination** — Requires coordination between Shipping, Railways, Environment, Customs
