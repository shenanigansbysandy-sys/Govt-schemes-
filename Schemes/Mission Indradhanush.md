# Mission Indradhanush

## Overview
A flagship immunization program aiming to achieve **full immunization coverage** (over 90%) for all children under 2 years and pregnant women against **12 Vaccine-Preventable Diseases (VPDs)** .

## Key Details
- **Target:** Children under 2 years & pregnant women
- **Vaccines Covered:** 12 VPDs including polio, measles, rubella, TB, diphtheria, tetanus, hepatitis B, pneumococcal, rotavirus
- **Phases:**
  - Mission Indradhanush (2014-2017) — 87% coverage
  - Intensified Mission Indradhanush (IMI) 1.0 (2017-2018)
  - IMI 2.0 (2019-2021)
  - IMI 3.0 (2021-2022)
  - IMI 4.0 + Intensified and Expanded IMI (2023-onwards)

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Full immunization coverage | ✅ ~90%+ (up from 65% in 2014) |
| Children vaccinated | ✅ 4+ crore children fully vaccinated through MI rounds |
| Pregnant women immunized | ✅ 1+ crore women vaccinated |
| Zero-dose children (unvaccinated) | 📉 Reduced from 35 lakh to ~8 lakh |
| Districts prioritized | 🔄 IMI 4.0 focuses on high-priority districts |

## Challenges
- **Last-mile delivery** — Reaching children in urban slums, remote villages, and conflict areas
- **Vaccine hesitancy** — Social media misinformation (especially post-COVID) around vaccine safety
- **Health worker fatigue** — ASHA workers are overburdened with multiple programs
- **Migration** — Seasonal migration of families makes tracking children difficult
- **Cold chain logistics** — Maintaining vaccine cold chain in remote areas remains challenging
