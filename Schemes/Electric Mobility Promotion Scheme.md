# Electric Mobility Promotion Scheme (PM E-DRIVE)

## Overview
An umbrella scheme for accelerating **Electric Vehicle (EV) adoption** in India, providing **demand-side subsidies** for e-2Ws, e-3Ws, e-4Ws, and **supply-side incentives** for EV charging infrastructure.

## Evolution
| Phase | Period | Budget | Subsidy Focus |
|-------|--------|--------|---------------|
| **FAME I** | 2015-2019 | ₹895 crore | Early EV adoption, pilots |
| **FAME II** | 2019-2024 | ₹10,000 crore | e-2W, e-3W, e-buses, charging infra |
| **EMPS/PM E-DRIVE** | 2024-2028 (extended) | ₹10,000+ crore | Rationalized subsidies; focus on buses & trucks |

## Key Details (PM E-DRIVE)
- **e-2W:** ₹5,000-₹10,000 subsidy (reduced from earlier ₹15,000)
- **e-3W:** ₹10,000-₹25,000 subsidy
- **e-4W:** Focus on fleet operators (e-taxis, corporate) — ₹15,000-₹50,000
- **e-Buses:** ₹20 lakh/bus subsidy focus
- **e-Trucks:** New category — pilot for electric freight
- **Charging Infrastructure:** 10,000+ public charging stations target

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Total EVs supported (all phases) | ✅ 25+ lakh |
| e-2Ws supported | ✅ 15+ lakh |
| e-3Ws supported | ✅ 5+ lakh |
| e-buses deployed | ✅ 8,000+ (under FAME & PM e-Bus Sewa) |
| Public charging stations | ✅ 15,000+ operational |
| EV penetration (2W) | 📈 ~7% of new 2W sales (up from 1% in 2022) |

## Challenges
- **Subsidy reduction impact** — Rationalization has slowed EV adoption growth
- **Charging infrastructure** — Still concentrated in top 10 cities; tier-2/3 coverage poor
- **Battery cost** — 30-40% of EV cost — import dependence on China for cells
- **Range anxiety** — Real-world range lower than claimed
- **Fire incidents** — EV battery fires (especially e-2Ws) have dented consumer confidence
- **End-of-life batteries** — No organized recycling infrastructure for lithium-ion batteries
- **PLI dependency mismatch** — Auto PLI not fully aligned with EV ecosystem needs
