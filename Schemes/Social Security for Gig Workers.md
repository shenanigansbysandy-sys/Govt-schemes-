# Social Security for Gig Workers

## Overview
A landmark social security initiative for **platform/gig workers** (Swiggy, Zomato, Uber, Ola, Urban Company, etc.) announced in **Budget 2025-26**, providing **health coverage** and **registration** for ~1 crore gig workers on the **e-Shram platform**.

## Key Details
- **Registration:** Gig workers register on **e-Shram** portal (already has 30+ crore unorganized workers)
- **Health Coverage:** PM-JAY-like health insurance (₹5 lakh) for gig workers & family
- **Coverage:** ~1 crore registered gig workers (initial target)
- **Eligibility:** Platform workers (delivery, ride-hailing, domestic services, freelance)
- **Portability:** Portable insurance — works across states
- **Funding:** Government + cess on digital platforms/platform contribution model
- **Additional benefits:** Accidental insurance linked to PMSBY

## Challenges
- **Platform identification** — Large number of small & unorganized platforms difficult to regulate
- **Classification dispute** — Legal battle: gig workers as "employees" vs. "independent contractors"
- **Funding sustainability** — Should government or platforms pay? Current model relies on government budget
- **Worker churn** — High turnover makes continuous insurance enrolment difficult
- **Coverage of informal gigs** — Many gig workers are on cash-based platforms not tracked by government
- **Awareness & trust** — Many gig workers distrust government registration; fear of tax implications
