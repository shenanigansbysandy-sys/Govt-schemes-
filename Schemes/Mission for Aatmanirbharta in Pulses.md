# Mission for Aatmanirbharta in Pulses

## Overview
Launched in **Budget 2025-26** to achieve **self-reliance in pulse production**, specifically targeting **Urad, Tur (Arhar), and Masoor** — three key pulses where India faces significant import dependence.

## Key Details
- **Objective:** Reduce import dependence on pulses (currently ~15% of consumption is imported)
- **Target Crops:** Urad (Black Gram), Tur/Arhar (Pigeon Pea), Masoor (Red Lentil)
- **Strategy:** Expand area under cultivation + improve yield through better seeds & practices

## Challenges
- **Weather dependency** — Pulses are largely rain-fed; erratic monsoons pose major risk
- **Price volatility** — Farmers shift away from pulses when market prices fall below MSP
- **Low productivity** — India's pulse yields (700-800 kg/ha) are far below global benchmarks (1,500+ kg/ha)
- **Storage & procurement** — Inadequate infrastructure for pulse procurement in many states
- **Import competition** — Cheap imports can undercut domestic production
