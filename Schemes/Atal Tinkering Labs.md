# Atal Tinkering Labs (ATL)

## Overview
A scheme to **foster curiosity, creativity, and innovation** among school students (Classes 6-12) by establishing **tinkering labs** with DIY kits, 3D printers, robotics, sensors, electronics, and programming tools — enabling students to learn by making.

## Key Details
- **Target (original):** 10,000 ATLs across schools
- **Budget 2025 Expansion:** Additional 50,000 ATLs in government schools
- **Each Lab:** ₹20 lakh grant (one-time) + ₹5 lakh/year O&M for 5 years
- **Hands-on:** Students design prototypes, participate in competitions (ATL Marathon)
- **Focus:** STEM learning, design thinking, 21st-century skills, problem-solving
- **Goal:** Build India's pipeline of future innovators, scientists, and entrepreneurs

## Challenges
- **Quality variance** — Some ATLs collect dust (equipment unused) due to lack of teacher motivation
- **Curriculum integration** — ATL activities not integrated into school timetable — treated as extracurricular
- **Principal support** — ATL success depends on principal's interest — many schools underutilize
- **Equipment maintenance** — 3D printers, sensors break down; no dedicated maintenance budget
- **Rural reach** — Most ATLs in urban areas; rural & remote schools have fewer ATLs (being addressed)
- **Continuity** — Mentorship ends after Class 12; students lack pathway to incubation
