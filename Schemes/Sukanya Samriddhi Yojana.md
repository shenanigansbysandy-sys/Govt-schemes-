# Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

## Overview
A **small savings scheme** for the **girl child** (under the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao umbrella) that offers a **high interest rate** (currently 8.2% p.a., tax-free), allowing families to save for a girl's **education & marriage expenses**.

## Key Details
- **Eligibility:** Girl child aged 0-10 years; one account per girl; maximum 2 girl children per family
- **Deposit Limit:** ₹250 minimum to ₹1.5 lakh/year maximum
- **Interest Rate:** 8.2% p.a. (FY2025-26) — tax-free under 80C
- **Maturity:** 21 years from account opening OR marriage after 18 years (whichever earlier)
- **Operational:** Post offices & most commercial banks
- **Withdrawal:** 50% can be withdrawn after 18 years for higher education
- **Accounts Opened:** 4+ crore accounts | ₹2+ lakh crore deposited

## Benefits
- **Tax-free returns** — Outstanding 8.2% interest (much higher than FDs)
- **Education fund** — Creates a dedicated corpus for girl's higher education
- **Emancipation fund** — Money controlled by girl herself after maturity
- **Promotes girls' value** — Family invests in girl child's future financially

## Challenges
- **Awareness gap** — Many rural families unaware despite high returns
- **Upper limit** — ₹1.5 lakh/year is too low for families wanting to save more
- **Two-child limit excludes twins** — Strict interpretation excludes twins of opposite gender
- **Delayed account opening** — Many parents delay beyond 10-year eligibility
- **Bank/post office reluctance** — Some post offices & banks discourage opening SSY accounts (low commission)
- **Inflation adjusted returns** — 8.2% is good but post-tax real returns need monitoring
