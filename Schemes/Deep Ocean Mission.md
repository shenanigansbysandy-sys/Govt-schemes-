# Deep Ocean Mission

## Overview
India's ambitious **deep-sea exploration & ocean technology mission** to develop capabilities for **manned deep submersibles**, deep-sea mining, biodiversity exploration, and climate change studies in the Indian Ocean.

## Key Components
| Component | Detail | Status |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Samudrayaan** (Manned Submersible) | 3-person submersible to 6,000m depth | 🔄 Under development — trials expected 2027 |
| **Deep-sea Mining** | Polymetallic nodules from 5,000m depth in Indian Ocean | 🔄 Technology development underway |
| **Ocean Biodiversity** | Deep-sea species collection & bio-prospecting | 🔄 Ongoing |
| **Climate Change** | Ocean observation systems for climate modelling | ✅ Operational — 4,000+ ocean sensors deployed |
| **Ocean Energy** | OTEC (Ocean Thermal Energy Conversion) pilot | 🔄 Pilot plant design phase |

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Matsya6000 (submersible) | 🔄 Hull fabrication complete; integration underway |
| 6,000m depth-rated ROV | ✅ Operational (subsea robotics) |
| Polymetallic nodule collection system | 🔄 Prototype tested at 1,000m |
| Ocean Climate sensors | ✅ 4,000+ Argo floats, RAMA buoys deployed |
| International seabed area | ✅ India has 75,000 sq km exploration area in Indian Ocean |

## Challenges
- **Technology gap** — Deep-sea (6,000m+) technology is highly complex; only 5 nations have manned submersibles
- **Cost overruns** — High-tech R&D projects face significant cost escalation
- **Trained manpower** — Shortage of deep-sea engineers and marine biologists
- **Mineral rights uncertainty** — International Seabed Authority (ISA) regulations for deep-sea mining still evolving
- **Environmental concerns** — Deep-sea mining could damage fragile benthic ecosystems
- **Delayed timelines** — Samudrayaan originally targeted 2024; now expected 2027-28
