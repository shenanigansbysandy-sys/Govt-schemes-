# PM FME (Formalization of Micro Food Processing Enterprises)

## Overview
A scheme to **formalize & upgrade the unorganized micro food processing sector** (2.5+ crore units) by providing credit, technology, skill training, and market linkages to **Individual micro-enterprises** and **SHG/FPO clusters**.

## Key Details
- **Target:** 2,00,000+ micro food processing units
- **Coverage:** All districts; special focus on ODOP (One District One Product) approach
- **Support:**
  - Credit-linked capital subsidy (35-50% of eligible project cost, max ₹10 lakh)
  - Seed capital for SHGs (₹40,000 per SHG member)
  - Branding & marketing support
  - Training & handholding
- **ODOP Focus:** Each district promotes 1-2 signature food products (e.g., Kashmir saffron, Kolhapur jaggery, Varanasi paan masala, Gujarat khaman)

## Challenges
- **Bank finance gaps** — Banks hesitant to lend to unorganized micro-enterprises without collateral
- **Technology adoption** — Many micro-units use outdated equipment; upgrading is costly even with subsidy
- **Quality compliance** — FSSAI compliance (labeling, hygiene, packaging) is a challenge for micro-units
- **Market access** — Competing with established brands; limited access to modern retail & e-commerce
- **Cluster development** — Creating ODOP clusters requires land, common infra, & collective action — slow progress
