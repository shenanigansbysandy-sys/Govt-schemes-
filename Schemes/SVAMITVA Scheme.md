# SVAMITVA Scheme

## Overview
A transformative scheme using **drone technology** to survey & map **rural inhabited (Abadi) land** in every village, providing legal **property cards / e-ownership documents** to rural homeowners, enabling them to use land as collateral for loans.

## Key Details
- **Technology:** High-resolution drone survey → GIS maps → Property cards
- **Coverage:** 6.5+ lakh villages (all India)
- **Output:** Each household gets a **Record of Rights (RoR)** for their Abadi (homestead) land
- **Integration:** Cards linked to DigiLocker & land records portal
- **Nodal Ministry:** Ministry of Panchayati Raj with Survey of India implementing

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Villages surveyed (drone) | ✅ 4+ lakh out of 6.5+ lakh |
| Property cards issued | ✅ 2+ crore |
| States with >80% coverage | ✅ 10 states (Gujarat, MP, UP, Karnataka, etc.) |
| States lagging | 🔄 NE states, Jharkhand, West Bengal slow |
| Linked to DigiLocker | ✅ Available digitally |
| Bank loans against SVAMITVA cards | 🔄 RBI has accepted; banks piloting |

## Challenges
- **State government cooperation** — Some states (WB, Kerala) initially resisted participation
- **Drone regulations** — DGCA clearances & restricted zones near borders slow down survey
- **Disputed land** — Property disputes cannot be resolved through SVAMITVA (civil court matters)
- **Data updating** — Survey is a one-time exercise; no legal framework for regular updates
- **Awareness** — Many villagers don't understand the value of property cards
- **Bank acceptance** — Banks in some states still reluctant to accept SVAMITVA cards as collateral
