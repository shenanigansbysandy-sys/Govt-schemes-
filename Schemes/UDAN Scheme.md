# UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) — Regional Connectivity Scheme

## Overview
A revolutionary scheme to **democratize air travel** by connecting **underserved & unserved airports**, making flying **affordable** for the common citizen through **subsidized fares** (₹2,500/hour of flight capped).

## Key Details
- **Mechanism:** Airlines get **Viability Gap Funding (VGF)** to operate on unviable regional routes
- **Airfare Cap:** ₹2,500 for 1-hour flight (50% seats) — subsidized by VGF
- **Airports Revived:** 93 airports, heliports, & water aerodromes operationalized
- **Phases:**
  - UDAN 1 (2017) — 5 routes, 27 airports
  - UDAN 2 — 73 routes, 36 airports
  - UDAN 3 — Seaplanes & heliports
  - UDAN 4 — NE states, hilly areas, islands
  - UDAN 5 (2024) — Focus on remote & strategic areas

## Challenges
- **Airline viability** — Several UDAN operators (Air Deccan, Zoom Air) have shut down or exited
- **Aircraft availability** — Shortage of small aircraft suitable for regional routes (ATR, Q400)
- **Airport infrastructure** — Revived airports need runway upgrades, navigation aids, security
- **High operating costs** — ATF prices & airport charges make VGF requirement very high
- **Route suspensions** — Multiple routes suspended due to low demand or airline failure
