# PM-PRANAM (PM Promotion of Alternate Nutrients for Agriculture Management)

## Overview
A scheme designed to **incentivize states to reduce chemical fertilizer usage** by promoting **alternate nutrients** like Nano Urea, organic fertilizers, bio-fertilizers, and compost. States that reduce chemical fertilizer consumption receive a share of the subsidy savings.

## Key Details
- **Mechanism:** States get grants based on fertilizer subsidy savings they help achieve
- **Focus:** Reduce urea, DAP, NPK overuse — promote Nano Urea, organic manure, bio-fertilizers
- **Incentive:** 50% of subsidy savings goes to states as grants for development

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| State MOUs signed | 🔄 Underway — several states opted in |
| Nano Urea adoption | ✅ Growing — IFFCO's Nano Urea widely adopted |
| Fertilizer consumption trend | 🔄 Modest reduction visible — urea consumption still high |
| State-wise targets | 🔄 Being set based on baseline consumption |

## Challenges
- **Farmer behavior** — Deep-rooted habit of using chemical fertilizers; reluctance to switch
- **Subsidy distortion** — Urea is heavily subsidized, making it cheaper than alternatives
- **Yield concerns** — Farmers fear yield drop if they reduce chemical fertilizer use
- **Awareness** — Limited knowledge about alternate nutrient sources among small farmers
- **Supply chain** — Inadequate availability of bio-fertilizers and organic inputs in many regions
