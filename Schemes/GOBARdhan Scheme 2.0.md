# GOBARdhan Scheme 2.0

## Overview
A waste-to-wealth scheme that converts **cattle dung, organic waste, and kitchen waste** into **biogas/CBG (Compressed Biogas)** and **organic fertilizer (Fertilizer-om-pore)** , creating a circular economy in rural areas.

## Key Details
- **GOBARdhan 1.0 (2018):** Focus on village-level biogas plants under Swachh Bharat
- **GOBARdhan 2.0 (2024):** Expanded to urban areas, scaled up to large CBG plants
- **Plants:** 500+ community biogas plants, 5,000+ household-level plants
- **Output:** Biogas for cooking + CBG for transport fuel + organic fertilizer
- **Market Linkage:** Oil marketing companies (OMCs) commit to purchase CBG for blending

## Challenges
- **Collection logistics** — Cattle dung collection from scattered households is labour-intensive
- **Dung availability** — Only households with 2+ cattle can sustain a household plant
- **Plant maintenance** — Many community plants have become defunct due to poor maintenance
- **CBG offtake** — CBG blending with PNG is not mandatory; OMCs reluctant to buy at floor price
- **Fertilizer marketing** — Competing with subsidized chemical fertilizers; farmers reluctant to pay for organic fertilizer
