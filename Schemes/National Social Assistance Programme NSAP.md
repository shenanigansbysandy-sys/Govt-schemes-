# National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP)

## Overview
India's flagship **social pension program** providing monthly financial assistance to **Below Poverty Line (BPL)** households who are **elderly, widows, or persons with disabilities**.

## Components
| Sub-scheme | Benefit (per month) | Target Group |
|------------|---------------------|--------------|
| **IGNOAPS** (Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme) | ₹200 (65-79 yrs), ₹500 (80+ yrs) | BPL elderly (60+ yrs) |
| **IGNWPS** (Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme) | ₹300 | BPL widows (40-59 yrs) |
| **IGNDPS** (Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme) | ₹300 | BPL persons with disabilities (18-79 yrs) |
| **NFBS** (National Family Benefit Scheme) | ₹20,000 (one-time) | BPL families on death of primary breadwinner |

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Total beneficiaries | ✅ 3+ crore across all sub-schemes |
| Elderly pensioners | ✅ 2+ crore |
| Widow pensioners | ✅ 40+ lakh |
| Disability pensioners | ✅ 30+ lakh |
| DBT integration | ✅ Direct transfer to Aadhaar-linked bank accounts (95% coverage) |

## Challenges
- **Very low pension amount** — ₹200-500/month is grossly inadequate for basic needs
- **Coverage gaps** — Many eligible BPL households are not enrolled
- **State supplementation** — States add their own contribution (some add ₹500-₹2,000), creating huge inequity
- **Delayed disbursement** — Pension often delayed by 2-3 months in many states
- **Exclusion errors** — BPL list errors mean many poor elderly/widows are left out
- **No inflation indexation** — Pension amount hasn't increased proportionally with inflation since launch
