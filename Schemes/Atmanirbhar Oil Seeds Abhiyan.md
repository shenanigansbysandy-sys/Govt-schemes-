# Atmanirbhar Oil Seeds Abhiyan

## Overview
A mission launched to **boost domestic edible oilseed production** and **reduce India's reliance on imported edible oils**. India imports ~55-60% of its edible oil consumption, costing ₹75,000-80,000 crore annually in foreign exchange.

## Key Details
- **Target Crops:** Mustard, Groundnut, Soybean, Sunflower, Sesame, Palm Oil
- **Strategy:** Area expansion + productivity improvement + processing infrastructure
- **Goal:** Reduce import dependence by 25-30% by 2030
- **National Palm Oil Mission:** Included as a sub-component for tropical palm cultivation in NE states & Andaman

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Area expansion under oilseeds | 🔄 Progressing — mustard acreage up 12% in 2025-26 |
| Seed distribution | ✅ High-yielding variety seeds being distributed |
| Palm oil plantation in NE | 🔄 Slow progress — farmer adoption challenges |
| Processing infrastructure | 🔄 Under development — oil mills & extraction units |

## Challenges
- **High import dependency** — Domestic production meets only 40-45% of demand
- **Yield gap** — Indian oilseed yields are 30-40% below global averages
- **Price competition** — Cheap imported palm oil from Indonesia/Malaysia undercuts domestic prices
- **Land constraints** — Oilseeds compete with food grains for acreage
- **Climate vulnerability** — Oilseed crops are highly sensitive to weather extremes
