# National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC)

## Overview
India's **comprehensive climate strategy** outlining national actions to mitigate & adapt to climate change, built around **8 National Missions** covering energy, water, agriculture, forests, and more.

## 8 National Missions
| Mission | Focus | Status |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **1. National Solar Mission** | 100 GW solar by 2030 | ✅ 80+ GW installed |
| **2. National Mission for Enhanced Energy Efficiency** | Energy savings through PAT scheme | ✅ Ongoing |
| **3. National Water Mission** | Water conservation, 20% reduction in use | 🔄 Ongoing |
| **4. National Mission for Sustainable Habitat** | Green buildings, urban waste | 🔄 Ongoing |
| **5. National Mission for Sustaining Himalayan Eco-system** | Glacier monitoring, Himalayan biodiversity | 🔄 Ongoing |
| **6. Green India Mission** | Forest cover increase, ecosystem restoration | 🆕 Revamped 2025 |
| **7. National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture** | Climate-resilient crops, water efficiency | 🔄 Ongoing |
| **8. National Mission on Strategic Knowledge for Climate Change** | Climate research & modelling | 🔄 Ongoing |

## Updated NAPCC (2025 revision)
- **NDC Target:** 45% reduction in emission intensity by 2030 (from 2005 levels)
- **Net Zero Pledge:** Net zero by 2070
- **Updated Targets:** 500 GW non-fossil energy capacity by 2030

## Challenges
- **Implementation deficit** — Missions lack concrete timelines & measurable milestones
- **Coordination gap** — 8 missions across 8+ ministries — limited inter-ministerial coordination
- **State-level disconnect** — States not adequately involved in NAPCC implementation
- **Funding constraints** — Climate action is largely co-financed through other schemes
- **No carbon tax** — India lacks economy-wide carbon pricing mechanism
- **Science-policy gap** — Limited use of climate modelling for policy decisions
