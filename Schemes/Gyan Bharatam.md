# Gyan Bharatam

## Overview
Announced in **Budget 2025-26**, this mission aims to **digitize over 1 crore manuscripts** from across India and create a **National Digital Repository** of India's civilizational knowledge, making it accessible to researchers, scholars, and the public.

## Key Details
- **Objective:** Preserve India's rich manuscript heritage (estimated 10+ million manuscripts) in digital form
- **Coverage:** Palm leaf, paper, birch bark, cloth manuscripts in 60+ ancient & regional languages
- **Repository:** National Digital Manuscripts Repository — freely accessible online
- **Languages:** Sanskrit, Pali, Prakrit, Tamil, Persian, Arabic, and regional languages

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Manuscripts identified | 🔄 Survey underway — 50 lakh identified so far |
| Digitization centres | 🔄 100+ centres established across states |
| Already digitized | ✅ ~15 lakh manuscripts digitized (from pre-existing efforts) |
| National portal design | ✅ Portal framework completed |
| New digitization target | 🆕 Additional 20 lakh/year for next 5 years |

## Challenges
- **Scale of work** — 1 crore manuscripts at high resolution is an enormous digitization task
- **Preservation vs. digitization** — Fragile manuscripts can be damaged during handling
- **Language expertise** — Lack of experts who can read & catalog ancient scripts & languages
- **Storage & sustainability** — Long-term digital preservation requires continuous investment
- **Access vs. conservation** — Balancing public access with need to protect cultural heritage
