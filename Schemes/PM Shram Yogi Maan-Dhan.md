# PM Shram Yogi Maan-Dhan (PM-SYM)

## Overview
A **voluntary contributory pension scheme** for **unorganized workers** (street vendors, rickshaw pullers, construction workers, domestic workers, agri labour, etc.) providing a **minimum monthly pension of ₹3,000** after 60 years of age.

## Key Details
- **Eligibility:** Unorganized workers aged 18-40 years with monthly income below ₹15,000
- **Contribution:**
  - Worker contributes ₹55-₹200/month (graduated by entry age)
  - Central Government contributes **matching amount**
- **Pension:** ₹3,000/month after 60 years
- **Family pension:** 50% of pension to spouse after worker's death
- **Exit option:** If exited before 60, worker gets their contribution + interest (government share forfeited back to corpus)
- **Beneficiary base:** 70+ lakh enrolled (as of 2026)

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Enrolments | ✅ 70+ lakh (target 10+ crore — largely unmet) |
| Enrolment centres | ✅ CSC network + Labour Department |
| Auto-debit facility | ✅ Enabled for bank account auto-debit |
| Awareness campaigns | 🔄 Limited — many workers still unaware |
| Monthly pension disbursement | 🔄 First set of beneficiaries reaching 60 now — payouts begun |

## Challenges
- **Very low enrolment relative to target** — 70L out of 40+ crore target (less than 2%)
- **Affordability** — Even ₹55-200/month is seen as burdensome for daily wage workers
- **Trust deficit** — Unorganized workers don't trust government schemes — fear of not getting money back
- **Awareness** — No mass awareness campaign unlike PM-KISAN or Jan Dhan
- **Competing schemes confusion** — PM-SYM, APY, PM-KMDY — workers confused about which to join
- **Bank linkage issues** — Many workers lack bank accounts or regular contribution capacity
