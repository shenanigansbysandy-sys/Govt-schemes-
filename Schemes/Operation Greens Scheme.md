# Operation Greens Scheme

## Overview
A price stabilization and value chain development scheme for **horticultural produce**, originally focused on **TOP crops** (Tomato, Onion, Potato), later expanded to include 22 more perishable horticultural crops.

## Key Details
- **Objective:** Reduce price volatility of perishable horticultural crops
- **Interventions:** 
  1. **Short-term:** Price stabilization through market intervention & controlled release
  2. **Long-term:** Building cold storage, supply chain infrastructure, FPOs
- **Eligible Crops:** Tomato, Onion, Potato + 22 additional perishable crops

## Implementation Progress (as of May 2026)
| Parameter | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Cold storage capacity added | 🔄 50+ new storage units operational |
| FPO linkages | ✅ Farmer Producer Organizations linked to markets |
| Price intervention during gluts | 🔄 Selective — budget constraints limit scale |
| Crop diversification support | 🔄 Underway |

## Challenges
- **Budget limitations** — Scheme budget is modest (~₹500 crore) relative to the scale of price volatility
- **Post-harvest losses** — India loses 10-15% of horticultural produce due to inadequate cold chain
- **Market fragmentation** — APMC mandis are state-controlled; fragmented supply chains
- **Timing of intervention** — Government often intervenes too late during price crashes
- **Storage gaps** — Cold storage capacity is concentrated in a few states (UP, West Bengal, Punjab)
